## The Four Tiers — Expanded

### TIER S: NEVER DEPICTED

The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), his wives (Mothers of the Believers), the Four Rightly Guided Caliphs (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali).

**WHAT THIS MEANS IN PRACTICE:**
- No visual depiction of any kind
- No invented dialogue under any circumstances
- No description of physical appearance
- No direct voice (unless authenticated quote used in narration with explicit source citation and chain strength)
- Referenced only through others' reactions and documented effects
- Their presence is felt through: empty seats, turned backs, the reactions of Tier B characters around them, letters and documents (shown as manuscripts), the consequences of their documented decisions

**THE ONE EXCEPTION:**
Authenticated hadith and documented historical quotes may be used in narration (not as dialogue) with explicit citation: "Al-Tabari records that Abu Bakr said to the community..." The quote is narrated, not performed.

### TIER A: SCHOLARLY CARE REQUIRED

Senior companions, members of Ahl al-Bayt, figures whose depiction requires consultation with Islamic scholarship.

**Specific Tier A figures and their individual rules:**

**HUSAYN IBN ALI:**
- Never depicted directly
- Karbala told entirely through Zaynab's perspective
- His last prayer referenced but not shown
- His death: Zaynab sees the aftermath, not the moment
- Physical presence: felt through others' grief and words

**HASSAN IBN ALI:**
- May be referenced in political context (abdication)
- His documented reasoning presented (preserved bloodshed)
- Not depicted in scenes — referenced through others
- His abdication document may appear as manuscript visual

**ZAYNAB BINT ALI:**
- May be shown in the aftermath of Karbala
- Her speech to Yazid is documented — may be used in narration
- Depicted with full dignity, scholarly care
- Her survival and testimony are the dramatic engine of Karbala arc
- Shia sources primary here — flagged as such

**AISHA BINT ABI BAKR:**
- Political actor in Season 4 — careful depiction allowed
- Battle of Camel: her documented role shown
- Her documented later regret MUST be included
- Never depicted in ways that could be read as criticism
- Both Sunni framing (legitimate political position) and her own documented reconsideration presented together

**BILAL IBN RABAH:**
- Close companion — Tier A
- His documented anguish after Prophet's death (broken adhan) is in sources — may be referenced carefully
- His voice (the adhan) is his defining characteristic
- Never depicted making decisions about the community's direction
- His role: witness, voice, human connection to loss

**HAMZA IBN ABD AL-MUTTALIB:**
- Already dead before the Rashidun era (Uhud, 625 CE)
- Referenced in Wahshi's redemption arc
- Never depicted — referenced through Wahshi's guilt

**FATIMAH AL-ZAHRA:**
- Died within months of the Prophet (PBUH)
- Referenced in Season 1 only through documented accounts
- Never depicted — her presence felt through others' grief

**SALMAN AL-FARSI:**
- Very close companion — Tier A despite Tier B-like accessibility
- May be shown in governance role (al-Mada'in governor)
- Does not reminisce about the Prophet directly on screen
- His perspective as Persian-born observer used carefully

### TIER B: FULL DEPICTION PERMITTED

All other companions, all Tabi'un, all later historical figures not in the Prophet's immediate inner circle.

Full cinematic depiction. Dramatized dialogue clearly framed as dramatic interpretation when not from primary sources. Cite specific historical claims.

**STILL REQUIRED even for Tier B:**
- Source attribution for specific historical claims
- Surfacing of scholarly debates rather than resolving them
- Acknowledgment of historical gaps ("history does not record...")
- Sensitivity to the figure's standing in Islamic tradition

---

### TIER C: CONTESTED FIGURES

Yazid ibn Muawiyah, Aurangzeb, Al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf, Nadir Shah, and others where significant scholarly disagreement exists about their character or Islamic standing.

**RULES:**
- Document actions — do not pass moral judgment on their Islam
- Acknowledge the scholarly dispute explicitly
- Give their stated reasoning where it exists in sources
- Never use as simple villains — complexity is the point
- Note which tradition holds which view
- Al-Hajjaj: both the enforcer of order AND the killer of scholars. Both are true. Show both.

## THE KARBALA PROTOCOL

Karbala (680 CE) is the most sensitive event in Islamic history. It divides Sunni and Shia Islam at the deepest level.

**MANDATORY RULES:**
1. Never take a position on the legitimacy question (Was Yazid's caliphate legitimate? Islam Stories does not answer.)
2. Always present both Sunni AND Shia accounts where they differ
3. Flag which account comes from which tradition
4. Husayn is never depicted directly
5. The story is Zaynab's — she is the witness and the voice
6. The secondary betrayal (Kufa's abandonment) is as important as the primary (Yazid's army) — both documented
7. Husayn's last words: documented in sources, cited with chain

**VISUAL RULES FOR KARBALA:**
- The plain: flat, exposed, no cover, hot October
- The water cut off: this is documented, must appear
- The 72: documented names in sources — not anonymous masses
- The aftermath: shown through Zaynab's eyes only

## THE FOUR-SOURCE CHECK

Before any specific claim appears in a script:

1. Which primary source documents this?
2. What is the chain strength (sahih/hasan/daif/scholarly)?
3. Do other sources agree or disagree?
4. If disagree — is the disagreement surfaced in the script?

If no primary source exists: the claim does not appear.
If the gap exists: "History does not record what happened next."
That sentence is more powerful than any invented detail.

---

## THE SCHOLARLY WISDOM SOURCE CHECK

Before any scholar citation appears in a script:

1. Is the scholar's name correctly transliterated?
2. Is the work title confirmed (Arabic + English)?
3. Is the specific chapter/volume/hadith number located?
4. Is the confidence flag accurate (SAHIH/HASAN/DA'IF/VERIFY)?
5. Does the quote or paraphrase faithfully represent the source?

All VERIFY items must be resolved before final audio production.
They do not block script generation or outline approval.
