# Islam Stories — Rashidun Character Guide
**Per-figure depiction rules for all ensemble characters**
**READ BY:** every Rashidun episode script generator call

---

## The Governing Principle

The Four Caliphs (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali) are **Tier S**.
They are **NEVER** depicted directly. They are felt through
their decisions, their documented words, and their effects
on the Tier B characters who carry the screen.

The drama of the era is **NOT** between the caliphs.
It is driven by third parties, structural forces,
and the impossible speed of empire-building.

---

## TIER S FIGURES — Handling Rules

### Abu Bakr al-Siddiq
**NEVER depicted.** Referenced as "the Caliph" or "Abu Bakr" by others.
His decisions are felt through their effects on Tier B figures.

**Key documented moments to reference (never depict):**
- His words on the day of the Prophet's death (Ibn Hisham)
- His decision to fight the Ridda Wars (Al-Tabari)
- His words to Khalid before dismissing him (Al-Tabari)
- His deathbed naming of Umar (Al-Tabari)

**Visual:** Never on screen. His voice may be heard as narration
if words are authenticated in primary sources with citation.

### Umar ibn al-Khattab
**NEVER depicted.** The most active Tier S presence in Season 2.
His decisions shape every Tier B character's arc.

**Key documented moments to reference (never depict):**
- The dismissal letter to Khalid (authenticated, two reasons documented)
- His personal journey to receive Jerusalem's surrender (Al-Tabari)
- His instructions to Abu Ubayda during the plague (Ibn Sa'd)
- His assassination during Fajr prayer (Al-Tabari — detailed account)

**Visual:** Never on screen. May be referenced visually by showing
his letter, his administrative decrees, reactions of others.

### Uthman ibn Affan
**NEVER depicted.** Season 3 gravitational center.

**Key documented moments to reference (never depict):**
- His compilation of the Quran (major achievement)
- His words during the siege: "I do not want to be the first
  caliph in whose time Muslim blood is shed" (documented)
- His death: reading Quran when rebels entered (multiple sources)
- His wife Na'ila losing fingers trying to shield him (documented)

**CRITICAL:** Never suggest Uthman conspired with the rebels
or was complicit in his own murder. The forged letter was
almost certainly Marwan's doing (scholarly consensus).

### Ali ibn Abi Talib
**NEVER depicted.** Season 4 gravitational center.

**CRITICAL ACCURACY POINTS:**
- Ali tried to **PROTECT** Uthman. His son Hasan was wounded
  guarding Uthman's house at Ali's request. (Al-Tabari)
- Ali convinced rebels NOT to cut off Uthman's water. (Ibn Sa'd)
- Ali was Uthman's guarantor with the rebels. (Al-Tabari)
- Ali did **NOT** conspire in Uthman's murder. Never imply otherwise.
- Ali at Siffin: insisted he would pursue justice for Uthman
  "in due course, probably after establishing his authority."
  His position was principled, not evasive. (Al-Tabari)

## TIER B FIGURES — Full Depiction Rules

### Khalid ibn Walid
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier B
**ERA PRESENCE:** Seasons 1, 2

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His conversion and what changed (and what didn't)
- His military genius — tactics documented in Al-Baladhuri
- The blood money incident at Banu Jadhima
- His dismissal mid-campaign — reading the letter, finishing
  the battle, handing over command
- His later life in Homs, dying in bed
- His documented last words (attributed, reliability disputed —
  MUST flag as "attributed to Khalid" not stated as fact)

**WHAT CANNOT BE INVENTED:**
- His inner thoughts during the conversion (not documented)
- Any personal animosity toward Umar or Abu Bakr
- Making him bitter about the dismissal (his documented
  response was acceptance, not bitterness)

**SCHOLARLY DEBATE TO SURFACE:**
Why Umar dismissed him: blood money incident OR fear of
hero worship. Al-Tabari records both. Script must present
both without resolving. Umar never publicly stated his reason.

### Abu Ubayda ibn al-Jarrah
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier B
**ERA PRESENCE:** Season 2

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His contrast with Khalid — humility vs brilliance
- Taking command after Khalid's dismissal
- His response to the plague: choosing to die with his army
- His documented words to his soldiers during the plague
- His death (639 CE, plague of Amwas)

**KEY CONTRAST TO DRAMATIZE:**
Two men, one battle (Yarmouk). Khalid: the weapon.
Abu Ubayda: the servant. Neither is superior —
they represent different answers to the same question
of what service looks like.

### Amr ibn al-As
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier B
**ERA PRESENCE:** Seasons 1, 2, 3, 4

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His conversion (calculated or genuine — sources disagree,
  SURFACE the debate, don't resolve it)
- The conquest of Egypt — his greatest achievement
- His recall by Uthman and his fury at being sidelined
- His defection to Muawiyah at Siffin
- The Qurans-on-spears tactic — DOCUMENTED as his idea
- His negotiation of Egypt as his price for supporting Muawiyah

**WHAT CANNOT BE INVENTED:**
- Making him a simple villain
- His son Abdullah's parallel story MUST appear when
  Amr appears in Season 4 — the contrast is the drama

**SCHOLARLY DEBATE:**
His conversion — pragmatic or genuine? His defection —
calculated self-interest or legitimate grievance?
The script presents both without choosing.

### Salman al-Farsi
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier A (close to Prophet, requires care)
**ERA PRESENCE:** Seasons 1, 2

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His role as governor of al-Mada'in (Ctesiphon)
- The Persian who now governs the Persian capital
- His perspective as an outsider watching the community
- His death in his province (circa 656 CE)

**WHAT REQUIRES CARE:**
His closeness to the Prophet (PBUH) means his memories
of that relationship are Tier S territory.
Show him governing, not reminiscing about the Prophet directly.

### Abu Dharr al-Ghifari
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier A (senior companion)
**ERA PRESENCE:** Seasons 2, 3

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His public criticism of wealth accumulation in Syria
- His documented clashes with Muawiyah over this
- His exile by Uthman to al-Rabadha
- His death alone in the wilderness (documented)
- His radical position on Islamic economic equality

**FRAMING:**
Both Abu Dharr and Uthman held principled positions.
Abu Dharr's position: wealth concentration violates Islam.
Uthman's position: governance requires administrative latitude.
Neither is the villain. Both believed they were right.
The exile is a tragedy, not a crime — present it as such.

### Muawiyah ibn Abi Sufyan
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier B (early career) / Tier C (after Siffin)
**ERA PRESENCE:** Seasons 3, 4 (transitions to Umayyad era)

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His administrative genius as governor of Syria
- His offer to give Uthman refuge in Damascus (refused)
- His legitimate grievance: his kinsman was killed while
  Muawiyah's requested reinforcements were ignored
- His political calculation at Siffin
- His use of the bloodstained shirt to rally Syria

**FRAMING (CRITICAL):**
Muawiyah is NOT a simple villain. His position at Siffin
had internal logic: avenge Uthman before giving bayah.
The script documents his reasoning. It also documents
the consequences. The audience draws conclusions —
the show does not hand them a verdict.

**SCHOLARLY DEBATE:**
Sunni tradition: Muawiyah was mistaken but not a rebel.
Shia tradition: open rebellion against legitimate authority.
Both positions presented explicitly. Neither endorsed.

### Marwan ibn al-Hakam
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier B/C (complex figure)
**ERA PRESENCE:** Season 3 (primary), transitions to Umayyad

**THIS IS THE KEY FIGURE THE SHOW MUST HANDLE CORRECTLY.**

Marwan is Uthman's secretary and cousin.
The forged letter that triggered the final siege is
attributed by scholarly consensus to Marwan, not Uthman.

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His growing control over Uthman's correspondence
- The intercepted letter — presented as his (scholarly consensus)
  with the caveat that some historians disagree
- His flight from Medina after the assassination
- His later emergence as Umayyad caliph (Season bridge)

**FRAMING:**
Marwan is the connective tissue between Rashidun fracture
and Umayyad consolidation. He outlived everyone.
He is not a mustache-twirling villain — he believed
in Umayyad legitimacy and acted accordingly.
His actions had catastrophic consequences.
That is enough drama without simplifying him.

### Ammar ibn Yasir
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier A (son of first martyrs, senior companion)
**ERA PRESENCE:** Seasons 1, 2, 3, 4

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His presence across all four caliphates
- His documented position on the Uthman crisis
- His role at Siffin — fighting for Ali
- His death at Siffin (documented)

**THE PROPHECY:**
The Prophet (PBUH) reportedly said Ammar would be killed
by the "transgressing party." He was killed at Siffin
by Muawiyah's forces. This is documented in hadith.
The script includes this — cited with source and chain strength.
It does not interpret it for the audience.
The audience interprets it themselves.

### Abdullah ibn Masud
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier A (close companion, scholar)
**ERA PRESENCE:** Seasons 2, 3

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- His role as keeper of a different Quran text
- His distress when Uthman recalled and destroyed his copies
- His documented objection to the standardization
- His death (circa 652 CE)

**FRAMING:**
His objection to Uthman's Quran standardization is
documented and historically important. The script presents
his position with sympathy — a man who memorized Quran
directly from the Prophet watching his version recalled.
It also presents why standardization was necessary.
Both are true. Neither cancels the other.

### Nusayba bint Ka'ab (Umm Amara)
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier B
**ERA PRESENCE:** Season 1 (Yamama, 632 CE)

In Season 1 context: Yamama is her final battle.
Her sons, her hand, her survival.
The crossover with Wahshi and Khalid at this battle.
See full Series 3 character arc for complete depiction rules.

### Wahshi ibn Harb
**DEPICTION:** Full — Tier B
**ERA PRESENCE:** Season 1 (Yamama)

His redemption arc completes here.
Killed Hamza at Uhud for Hind.
Accepted Islam at the Conquest of Mecca.
Kills Musaylima at Yamama.
His documented words: "I killed the best of men and
the worst of men." (Ibn Sa'd — chain disputed, flag accordingly)

**SCHOLARLY WISDOM REQUIREMENT:**
This arc requires tawbah treatment per the Scholarly Wisdom
Standard. See `islam_stories_scholarly_wisdom_standard.md`.
Reference S1E1 "The Same Spear" as the template implementation.

## TIER A FIGURES — Special Handling

### Aisha bint Abi Bakr
**TIER A** — Major political actor, requires scholarly care
**ERA PRESENCE:** Season 4 (Battle of Camel)

**WHAT CAN BE SHOWN:**
- Her documented position: justice for Uthman required
  immediate punishment of the killers
- The Battle of the Camel — she was there, she led, documented
- Her documented later regret: authenticated in sources

**WHAT REQUIRES CARE:**
- Never depicted making decisions without source authentication
- Her closeness to the Prophet (PBUH) means Tier S proximity
- The Battle of Camel is controversial across traditions
- Both Sunni and Shia sources used — flagged appropriately

**MUST INCLUDE:**
Her documented later regret. This is not to diminish her —
it is to show her integrity. She reconsidered. She said so.
That is in the sources. The script includes it.

## WHAT EVERY RASHIDUN SCRIPT MUST DO

1. **Name the actual drivers of each crisis**
   (Marwan for S3, rebel factions for S3-4, Muawiyah for S4)
2. **Show Ali and Uthman as what they were — allies,
   not opponents.** Ali tried to protect Uthman.
3. **Present scholarly debates explicitly.**
   Two positions. Two sources. No resolution.
4. **Show the caliphs' humanity through others' reactions,
   documented words, and effects of their decisions —
   never through direct depiction.**
5. **Attribute every specific claim to its primary source.**
   "Al-Tabari records..." "Ibn Sa'd documents..."
   "The sources agree on X but disagree on Y."
6. **Include 2–3 scholarly wisdom moments per episode.**
   See `islam_stories_scholarly_wisdom_standard.md`.
7. **End every episode on the question, not the answer.**
