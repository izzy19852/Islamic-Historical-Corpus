*Locked decisions, architecture, and build order*

## 1. The Vision

A cinematic Islamic civilizational history channel covering 1,400 years across the Middle East, South Asia, Africa, Europe, and Eastern Europe — told from **primary Islamic sources**, not Western colonial narratives.

**Positioning:** Fall of Civilizations depth + Kings and Generals battle structure + Kling 3.0 cinematic visuals + moral complexity that no existing channel provides.

**The one-paragraph pitch:**
Islam Stories is the channel that treats Islamic civilization the way Fall of Civilizations treats every other civilization — with literary immersion, primary source depth, and the courage to sit with moral complexity. The visual language is rooted in Islamic aesthetics: warm desert light, Arabic calligraphy, the actual geography of the battles. The music is authentic — oud and ney, not generic orchestral. The narration immerses rather than explains. The sourcing — Al-Tabari, Al-Waqidi, Ibn Hisham — gives it things no other channel can say.

**Scope:** Post-Prophet (PBUH) — 632 CE through Ottoman decline (~1900 CE)

**Dual audience:** Muslim core audience + non-Muslim history enthusiasts

## 4. Visual Identity (LOCKED)

### Three-Mode Visual System

| Mode | Style Reference | When Used | Color Grade |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Character/Narrative** | Kingdom of Heaven / HBO | Personal moments, decisions, character reveals | Location-specific warm tones |
| **Battle/Action** | Snyder visual grammar, Islamic palette | Episode climax, charges, clashes | Deep amber, dust gold, God-rays |
| **Geographic/Establishing** | Planet Earth / NatGeo | Episode opens, act transitions, geography | True-to-location natural light |
| **Primary Source Quotes** | Manuscript aesthetic | Direct quotes from Al-Tabari, Ibn Hisham, etc. | Dark/black + Arabic calligraphy |

### Per-Series Color Palette
| Series Geography | Color Grade |
|---|---|
| Arabian Peninsula / Levant | Warm amber, golden dust, deep terracotta |
| Persia / Central Asia | Grey-green, silver, cool earth tones |
| Andalusia / Mediterranean | Blue-teal, warm terracotta, golden stone |
| Africa (Sahel / Mali) | Rich ochre, deep green, copper tones |
| Ottoman / Anatolian | Deep blue, gold, marble white |

### Kling Prompt Templates (LOCKED)

**Mode 1 — Character/Narrative:**
```
cinematic documentary style, natural golden hour lighting,
warm amber tones, [location] environment, intimate [shot type],
dust particles in air, period-accurate [costume/armor],
no stylization, Kingdom of Heaven aesthetic, no blood no gore
```

**Mode 2 — Battle/Action:**
```
cinematic slow motion battle scene, low angle shot tilting up,
golden dust backlit by setting sun, God-rays through dust cloud,
[army description] in background, epic scale, motion blur on weapons,
deep amber color grade, no blood no gore, Snyder cinematography style
```

**Mode 3 — Geographic/Establishing:**
```
vast aerial establishing shot, [specific location] landscape at
golden hour, cinematic wide angle lens, atmospheric haze on horizon,
epic geographical scale, no people visible, Planet Earth cinematography,
[specific terrain features]
```

**Mode 4 — Primary Source Quote:**
*Not a Kling clip — FFmpeg treatment:*
- Arabic manuscript texture background
- Arabic text appears first (calligraphy style)
- English translation fades in below
- Silence or sparse oud underneath

### Styles Rejected and Why
- **Studio Ghibli/Anime:** Tonally wrong for grave historical material. Reserve for future kids' spinoff.
- **Witcher Dark Fantasy:** "Fictional world" signal undermines historical credibility. Use only for European/Crusades-from-Christian-side scenes.
- **Superman Returns / Man of Steel:** Too modern/Western, wrong color temperature for Islamic geography.
- **Single-style approach:** All competitors do this. The three-mode system creates emotional range they lack.

## 5. Audio Identity (LOCKED)

### Voice
- **Register:** Authoritative, warm, slightly melancholic — storyteller, not preacher, not lecturer
- **Reference:** Paul Cooper (Fall of Civilizations) gravity applied to Islamic history
- **Requirements:** Handles Arabic names correctly, works for long-form narration without fatigue
- **Tool:** ElevenLabs — audition "Daniel," "Liam," or custom voice clone
- **Decision needed:** Audition 10 voices reading 2 paragraphs of Islamic history before build

### Music (LOCKED)
- **Base layer:** Oud and ney (Arabic flute) — authentic instruments of the civilizations depicted
- **Build layer:** Strings and light percussion for dramatic sequences
- **Battle layer:** Full orchestral build over oud base
- **Silence:** Used deliberately during primary source quote moments — words breathe alone
- **Era-specific variation:**
  - Arabian/early Islam → classical Arabic oud, ney
  - Andalusia → Andalusian muwashshah influenced
  - Ottoman → makam-influenced strings
  - Africa → kora, balafon, West African percussion
  - Persia/Central Asia → tar, setar, Persian ney
- **Source:** Epidemic Sound, Artlist, Pixabay Islamic/Middle Eastern instrumental playlists

### Audio Pipeline
- Audio generated FIRST — this is the master timeline
- All video scenes cut to fit audio duration, not the reverse
- Narration always dominant — music at -20 to -25 dB under narration, -8 to -12 dB during battle sequences

```

    ↓

    ↓

    ↓

~$0.06 per episode in API costs
```

## 7. Script Generation System

### Three Prompts Required

**Prompt 1 — Research Brief:**
Generates structured episode brief before scripting:
- Protagonist, era, battle/event
- Primary sources available in RAG for this topic
- Conflicting accounts to surface
- Supporting characters
- The dramatic question (what's at stake)
- Known vs disputed facts

**Prompt 2 — Episode Script:**
Full cinematic script with tagged scene types:
```
[NARRATION] spoken text — Fall of Civilizations register
[SCENE_TYPE] kling_battle | kling_narrative | kling_establishing |
             ai_map | still_image | text_overlay | manuscript_quote
[VISUAL_PROMPT] complete Kling-ready prompt for this scene
[DURATION_EST] approximate seconds from narration length
[SOURCE_NOTE] which primary source this claim comes from
[CONFLICT_FLAG] where accounts diverge — surface as drama not footnote
```

**Prompt 3 — Visual Prompt Refinement:**
Pass over script to ensure every [VISUAL_PROMPT] is a proper Kling prompt with:
- Subject + action
- Setting + environment + time of day
- Camera language (angle, movement, distance)
- Mode-appropriate style tag
- Safety language (no blood, no gore)
- Consistency anchors if character appeared before

### Script Generation System Prompt (LOCKED)

```
You are the writer for Islam Stories, a cinematic historical 
documentary series covering Islamic civilization 632–1900 CE.

VOICE: Fall of Civilizations literary register — immersive, 
present-tense narration, sensory detail, human scale.
NOT a lecture. NOT a Wikipedia summary.

EPISODE STRUCTURE:
Act 1 — The World: sensory opening, geographic establishment
Act 2 — The Person: primary source details revealing character
Act 3 — The Conflict: political + military + personal stakes
Act 4 — The Battle/Event: cinematic centerpiece, sourced to 
         specific narrations
Act 5 — The Aftermath: immediate consequence + long arc
Act 6 — The Mystery: disputes, unknowns, scholarly tension

CITATION RULES:
- Every specific claim references its source:
  "Al-Tabari records..." / "Ibn Hisham notes..."
- When sources conflict: present both and name them
- Never smooth over disputes — they are dramatic gold
- Flag weak narrations: "A disputed account suggests..."
- Flag gaps: "History is silent on why..."

FORBIDDEN:
- Hagiography — no one is perfectly righteous
- Demonization — no one is purely evil
- Western colonial framing
- Unattributed claims
- Wikipedia-level surface summary

OUTPUT FORMAT per scene:
[NARRATION] — spoken text
[SCENE_TYPE] — visual type
[VISUAL_PROMPT] — complete generation prompt
[DURATION_EST] — approximate seconds
[SOURCE_NOTE] — citation
[CONFLICT_FLAG] — where accounts diverge
```

## 8. Production Pipeline (LOCKED)

### Full Flow
```
1. RESEARCH BRIEF (Claude + RAG)
   Topic → RAG query → structured brief
        ↓
2. EPISODE SCRIPT (Claude + RAG chunks)
   Brief + source material → full tagged script
        ↓
3. VISUAL PROMPT REFINEMENT (Claude)
   Script → refined Kling-ready prompts per scene
        ↓ (parallel tracks)
   ┌────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ 4. VOICEOVER (ElevenLabs)          │
   │    Narration text → audio file     │
   │    → master timeline established   │
   └────────────────────────────────────┘
        ↓
5. ASSET PRODUCTION (all timed to master timeline)
   ├── Kling video clips (8-10 per episode)
   │     battle/narrative/establishing scenes
   ├── Kling image generation (maps, portraits)
   │     → Ken Burns motion via FFmpeg
   ├── Public domain stills + manuscripts
   │     → Ken Burns motion via FFmpeg  
   └── Text overlays (primary source quotes)
        ↓
6. MUSIC LAYERING
   Oud/ney base + era-specific instruments
   Mixed against narration (narration dominant)
        ↓
7. ASSEMBLY (FFmpeg)
   Master audio + all scene clips in timeline order
   + music layer (ducked)
   + transitions between scenes
        ↓
8. SUBTITLES
   Whisper transcription → SRT → burn in
        ↓
9. POST PRODUCTION
   Thumbnail (best Kling frame + title text)
   YouTube metadata (Claude generates)
   Chapters (every major section timestamped)
        ↓
10. DISTRIBUTION
    YouTube upload + Shorts extraction
    (Best 60s Kling clip → 9:16 reformat → Shorts/Reels/TikTok)
```

## 15. Theological Boundaries and Sensitivity Framework

### The Constraint
Visual depiction of the Prophet (PBUH), his wives, and the Four Rightly Guided Caliphs is impermissible under the overwhelming majority of Islamic scholarly opinion. This is non-negotiable for Islam Stories to have credibility with the Muslim community it serves.

**The model:** Moustapha Akkad's *The Message* (1976) — the entire film depicts the era of the Prophet (PBUH) without ever showing him, voicing him, or putting the camera where he stands. It won international awards. It works. Islam Stories uses the same approach.

### Sensitivity Tiers

**Tier S — Never Depicted Visually**
- Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)
- All wives of the Prophet — Mothers of the Believers (Khadijah, Aisha, Hafsah, Umm Salamah, Safiyya, Maymunah, Zaynab bint Jahsh, Juwayriyya, Umm Habiba, Sawda, Ramlah)
- The Four Rightly Guided Caliphs: Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, Umar ibn al-Khattab, Uthman ibn Affan, Ali ibn Abi Talib

*Treatment: Referenced in narration, quoted from authenticated hadith and seerah. Their eras depicted through the companions and events around them. No Kling generation, no portrait, no voice acting as these figures. Third-person narration only.*

**Tier A — Handle with Scholarly Care**
Senior Companions (Sahabah) whose depiction requires sensitivity:
- Husayn ibn Ali — extreme sensitivity, central to Sunni/Shia theological divide, Karbala
- Hassan ibn Ali — same sensitivity
- Bilal ibn Rabah, Salman al-Farsi, Abdullah ibn Masud, Ammar ibn Yasir
- Any figure directly tied to the Fitna (first Islamic civil war)

*Treatment: Can be referenced in atmospheric/crowd scenes. Never isolated as the sole visual subject. Narration-led. Acknowledge both Sunni and Shia perspectives where relevant.*

**Tier B — Standard Historical Depiction**
Post-companion era figures and scholars:
- Khalid ibn Walid (companion, but less contested — handle with reverence)
- Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz (Umar II)
- Salah al-Din, Baybars, Muhammad al-Fatih, Omar al-Mukhtar
- Ibn Khaldun, Ibn Rushd, Ibn Sina, Al-Ghazali
- All rulers, generals, scholars, and resistance figures post-661 CE
- All women of Islam series figures

*Treatment: Full cinematic depiction. Kling video and image generation appropriate.*

**Tier C — Contested Figures (Present Multiple Perspectives)**
- Yazid ibn Muawiyah — Karbala, deeply controversial across communities
- Al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf — condemned by many, defended by some scholars
- Timur (Tamerlane) — claimed Islamic legitimacy, massacred Muslim cities
- Aurangzeb — revered and condemned across South Asian communities
- Any figure where Sunni/Shia or sectarian perspectives diverge significantly

*Treatment: State the dispute explicitly. Quote both traditions' sources. Never take a sectarian position. Let the audience sit with the tension.*

### Karbala Protocol
The single most theologically sensitive event in Islamic history. Husayn ibn Ali, grandson of the Prophet (PBUH), killed with most of his family at Karbala (680 CE).

- Sunni primary sources: Al-Tabari's account — political betrayal, tragic circumstances
- Shia primary sources: The event as sacred martyrdom, foundational to Shia theology
- Approach: Present both accounts explicitly. State that perspectives diverge fundamentally and why. Do not depict Husayn visually (Tier A). Lead with the human tragedy that both traditions acknowledge.
- The channel that handles Karbala honestly and fairly — citing both — will be trusted by both communities. This is a content position no other channel occupies.

### Editorial Position (LOCKED)
Islam Stories does not adjudicate theological disputes. It presents what the sources say, where they agree, and where they diverge. The audience decides. This is not relativism — it is intellectual honesty about the limits of historical certainty.

### 17.11 Islam Stories-Specific SEO Rules

**Dual audience keyword strategy:**

Never use in titles/descriptions/tags:
- Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) as a hook when episode doesn't depict him

- Anything that signals devotional/religious content when content is historical

**Language targeting:**

Always English titles and descriptions. YouTube distributes English globally. Do not include Arabic text in description — confuses algorithm categorization. Episode transcripts (auto-generated by Whisper) handle multilingual search indexing automatically.

### Kids Script Generation System Prompt

```
You write for "Islam Stories Kids" — an animated Islamic history
series for ages 6-12, in the style of Avatar: The Last Airbender.

VOICE: Warm, energetic, present-tense. Short sentences. Active
verbs. Words a 10-year-old understands without dumbing down.

EPISODE STRUCTURE (5-8 min, ~800 words narration):
- Hook (0:00-0:30): Drop into the action. No context-setting.
  "[Figure] is running. Behind him, an entire army."
- Who is this? (0:30-1:30): One paragraph. Who are they,
  why do we care, what do they want?
- The Challenge (1:30-3:30): The obstacle. What stands between
  them and what they want? Make it feel impossible.
- The Turning Point (3:30-5:30): How do they solve it?
  Through cleverness, courage, or character — never just
  strength alone. This is where the moral lives.
- The Lesson (5:30-6:30): What did we learn? One sentence
  from the hero or narrator. Should feel earned, not preachy.
- The Tease (last 30 sec): What happens next? Leave them
  wanting the next episode.

RULES:
- One hero, one challenge, one lesson per episode
- Hero must have a visible flaw they work through
- Violence: swords clash, armies meet — no injury shown,
  no death depicted, consequence-free action
- Never depict Tier S or Tier A figures (same rules as adult)
- No theological disputes — single age-appropriate narrative
- End every episode with something the child can DO or FEEL:
  courage, cleverness, patience, loyalty

FORBIDDEN:
- Graphic violence or consequences
- Theological complexity (save for adult channel)
- Long sentences (max 15 words)
- Words requiring dictionary for 10-year-old
- Hadiths or scholarly citations onscreen
  (source the facts, don't show the sourcing)
```
