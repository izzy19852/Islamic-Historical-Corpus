# Islam Stories — Scholarly Wisdom Standard
# INJECTED INTO EVERY EPISODE SCRIPT GENERATOR CALL
# Non-negotiable. Applies to all episodes without exception.

---

## What Is Required

Every episode script must include 2–3 moments where the narrator
surfaces named Islamic scholarly wisdom relevant to the story's
central dramatic question. These are woven into the existing
narration — not appended as a separate segment, not a lecture,
not a moral tacked on at the end.

The story earns the wisdom first. Then the narrator delivers it.

---

## The Three Placement Rules

### Placement 1 — The Theological Hinge
The moment in the story where the core Islamic concept first
becomes directly relevant to the character's situation.
The narrator surfaces the scholarly tradition on that concept.
Named scholar. Named work. Cited with chain confidence.

Examples by concept:
- Tawbah: when the character converts or seeks forgiveness
- Sabr: when the character faces an impossible wait or loss
- 'Adl: when the character must choose justice over loyalty
- Tawakkul: when the character acts despite certain defeat
- Shura: when the community must decide without their leader

### Placement 2 — The Human Struggle
The moment the character faces an impossible choice or an
unresolvable internal tension. The narrator draws on scholarly
wisdom about the human condition in Islam — not to resolve the
tension, but to deepen it with 'ilm. The audience feels the
weight of what Islam has said about this exact kind of moment.

### Placement 3 — The Closing Frame
The final narration closes with the relevant scholarly framework
and the explicit epistemic position: "Allah knows best" on
questions that cannot be resolved by historical evidence alone.
This is not a hedge. It is correct Islamic 'aqeedah and must be
presented with full weight — as the honest and honorable answer.

---

## Citation Format — Mandatory

Every named scholar citation must include:
- Scholar's full name (correct transliteration)
- Work title: Arabic transliterated + English translation
- Specific location where possible (chapter, volume, hadith number)
- Confidence flag: [SAHIH / HASAN / DA'IF / VERIFY]

### Example in narration:
"Ibn al-Qayyim, in Madarij al-Salikin — Stations of the Travelers
— writes that tawbah has three inseparable conditions: nadam,
genuine remorse; iqla', immediate cessation; and 'azm, firm
resolve never to return. [VERIFY: Madarij, chapter on tawbah]"

### Example scene block:
[SCENE_TYPE] manuscript_scholar
[SCHOLAR] Ibn al-Qayyim al-Jawziyya
[WORK] Madarij al-Salikin (Stations of the Travelers)
[CITATION] Chapter on tawbah [VERIFY specific location]
[CONFIDENCE] VERIFY
[VISUAL_PROMPT] Lamp-lit scholar's study, stacked books in Arabic
script, open manuscript showing dense theological text, ink and
parchment, warm amber candlelight, deep shadows, single light
source, classical Islamic scholarship aesthetic

---

## The "Allah Knows Best" Protocol

Any episode addressing the following MUST close with the explicit
scholarly position that certainty belongs to Allah alone:
- A person's standing with Allah
- The validity of their tawbah or iman
- What awaits them in the akhira
- The theological meaning of a historical event

Script template for closing:
"On that question, the greatest scholars of Islam offer only
the answer that is also the most honest answer: Allahu Akbar.
Allah knows best. That reckoning belongs to the Most Merciful
alone."

This closes every episode that touches these questions.
It is not a disclaimer. It is the correct epistemic position.

## The manuscript_scholar Scene Type

A variant of the existing manuscript_quote scene type.
Use specifically when a named scholarly text is being cited.

Required fields:
[SCENE_TYPE] manuscript_scholar
[SCHOLAR] Full name of scholar
[WORK] Arabic title transliterated + English translation
[CITATION] Location in work (chapter/volume/page if known)
[CONFIDENCE] SAHIH / HASAN / DA'IF / VERIFY

Visual register: lamp-lit scholar's study or library.
Books stacked. Ink and parchment. The specific text open.
Warm amber candlelight. Region-appropriate palette.
This signals to the audience: we are now in 'ilm.
The transition is intentional and honorable.

---

## What Scholarly Wisdom Is NOT

- Not a moral lesson appended at the end of the episode
- Not anonymous "Islamic tradition says..." without attribution
- Not a fatwa or ruling passed on the historical figure
- Not an interruption of the cinematic register
- Not the narrator telling the audience what to think

The wisdom enriches the drama. It does not replace it.
The scholars speak because the story has earned their voice.

---

## Pre-Production Checklist — Scholarly Citations

Every VERIFY-flagged citation requires confirmation before
production. Add to the episode's pre-production checklist:

- [ ] Scholar name spelled correctly (confirm transliteration)
- [ ] Work title confirmed (Arabic + English)
- [ ] Specific chapter/page/hadith number located
- [ ] Chain strength assessed and confidence flag updated
- [ ] Quote or paraphrase confirmed against the source text

VERIFY items do not block script generation.
They block final audio and visual production.
